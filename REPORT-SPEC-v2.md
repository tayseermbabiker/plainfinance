# PlainFinancials Report Spec v2
## Universal Structure + Industry Adaptation

---

## Philosophy

Every business owner asks the same 5 questions:
1. Did I make money?
2. Where is my cash?
3. Can I pay my bills?
4. What should I worry about?
5. What should I do this week?

The report answers these 5 questions for every industry. The STRUCTURE is fixed. The CONTENT adapts.

---

## What is FIXED (same for all industries)

### Section 1: Header
- Company name
- Period (month/year)
- Industry tag (e.g., "Restaurant / Food", "SaaS / Digital")
- Overall status badge: Safe / Watch / Danger

### Section 2: One-Sentence Headline
- AI-generated, industry-aware
- Example restaurant: "Revenue is strong but food cost is eating your profit and cash is dangerously low."
- Example SaaS: "MRR is growing but burn rate gives you only 4 months of runway."
- Colour-coded to match status (green/amber/red border)

### Section 3: 4 Pillars (at-a-glance)
- Always 4 cards in a 2x2 grid
- Pillar 1: **Revenue** (always)
- Pillar 2: **Key Cost Metric** (adapts per industry — see below)
- Pillar 3: **Cash Position** (always — cash in bank + context)
- Pillar 4: **Bottom Line** (net margin or net profit — always)
- Each pillar shows: label, big number, subtitle, status badge, one-line insight

### Section 4: P&L Summary
- Revenue
- [Industry COGS Label] (e.g., "Food & Beverage Cost", "Material Cost", "Cost of Service")
- Gross Profit + Gross Margin %
- Operating Expenses (OPEX)
- EBITDA
- Net Income + Net Margin %
- Industry benchmark bars for Gross Margin and Net Margin

### Section 5: AI Insight (plain English)
- AI-generated interpretation of the P&L
- Uses industry language
- References specific numbers
- Compares to industry benchmarks

### Section 6: Cash Bridge ("Where did your cash go?")
- Starting Cash
- + Net Profit
- +/- Receivables change
- +/- Inventory change
- +/- Payables change
- - Loan Repayments
- - Owner Drawings
- - Asset Purchases
- = Ending Cash
- Gap note if calculated doesn't match actual

### Section 7: Working Capital Health
- DSO, DIO, DPO cards (with industry-appropriate labels)
- Cash Conversion Cycle
- Working Capital Requirement table (actual vs industry target)
- AI insight on what's tying up cash

### Section 8: Cash Runway
- Big number (months or days depending on industry)
- Benchmark bar vs industry target
- Cash-out date if critical
- AI interpretation

### Section 9: Debt Coverage (if loans exist)
- FCF vs Loan Repayments
- Coverage ratio
- AI insight (tone matches overall status)

### Section 10: Bank Meeting Summary
- Ready-to-use sentence
- AI-generated with industry context

### Section 11: 3 Actions This Week
- Urgency tagged: Do Today / This Week / This Month
- AI-generated with industry-specific language
- Physical, actionable, with specific numbers

### Section 12: Non-Financial Investigation Points
- 3-5 items the owner should check that we can't calculate from P&L
- Industry-specific (see table below)

### Section 13: Footer
- Disclaimer
- Generated date
- PlainFinancials branding

### E. Investigation Points (non-financial, per industry)

| Industry | Investigate These |
|----------|-------------------|
| Restaurant | Food waste %, portion control, table turnover, menu item profitability, average check size |
| Retail | Shrinkage rate, sell-through rate, foot traffic, return rate, customer conversion |
| E-commerce | Churn rate, CAC, conversion rate, return rate, cart abandonment |
| SaaS | Monthly churn, LTV/CAC ratio, NRR, activation rate, support ticket volume |
| Services | Utilization rate, realization rate, client concentration, scope creep, project overruns |
| Construction | WIP as % of revenue, retention held, variation capture rate, subcontractor margin |
| Manufacturing | Yield/scrap rate, machine utilization, BOM accuracy, defect rate |
| Wholesale | Order fill rate, backorder rate, freight cost %, customer credit risk |
| Healthcare | Provider utilization, claim denial rate, revenue per visit, payer mix |
| General | Customer concentration, revenue per employee, repeat rate, acquisition cost |

### F. Action Triggers (what we CAN calculate → industry-specific advice)

**Universal triggers (all industries):**
- Gross Margin below industry danger threshold → margin advice
- Cash < Accounts Payable → liquidity advice
- DSO above industry warning → collections advice
- DIO above industry warning → inventory advice
- DPO below 20 days → supplier terms advice
- Cash runway below industry minimum → cash preservation advice
- Owner drawings > 40% of net profit → drawings warning

**Industry-specific triggers (from Perplexity action library):**
- Restaurant: COGS % > 36% → "Re-cost menus, enforce portion control, negotiate supplier pricing"
- Retail: DIO > 90 → "Identify top 5 slow movers, mark down or bundle to clear"
- SaaS: GM < 50% → "Optimize hosting, reduce manual support, rationalize features"
- Services: GM < 35% → "Adjust rates, replace contractors with employees, standardize playbooks"
- Construction: DSO > 75 → "Tighten credit control, require deposits, escalate late payers"
- Manufacturing: DIO > target → "Reduce batch sizes, implement pull-based planning, clear obsolete stock"
- Wholesale: DSO > 50 → "Tighten credit limits, offer early-payment discounts, enforce holds"
- Healthcare: DSO > 60 → "Strengthen claim accuracy, resolve denial reasons, follow-up protocols"

## What the AI generates (via OpenAI prompt)

The AI receives ALL calculated metrics + industry benchmarks + industry vocabulary.

It generates:
1. **HEADLINE** — one sentence, industry-aware
2. **P&L_INSIGHT** — 2-3 short blocks interpreting the P&L in industry language
3. **CASH_INSIGHT** — 2 sentences about cash cycle
4. **ACTION_1, ACTION_2, ACTION_3** — title + description, industry-specific, physical
5. **BANK_SUMMARY** — 2-3 sentences for bank/investor
6. **BENCHMARK_NOTE** — one sentence comparing to industry

The AI does NOT generate:
- Pillar configs (hardcoded per industry — more consistent)
- Investigation points (hardcoded per industry — more reliable)
- Benchmark numbers (calculated from data — exact)
- Cash bridge (calculated from data — exact)

## Inputs Needed

### Universal (all industries):
- Company name, industry, month/year, currency
- Revenue
- COGS (label adapts per industry)
- Operating Expenses (OPEX)
- Net Profit
- Cash in Bank
- Opening Cash (start of year) — for cash bridge
- Accounts Receivable
- Inventory
- Accounts Payable
- Short-term Loans
- Other Liabilities
- Loan Repayments
- Owner Drawings
- Asset Purchases
- Planned Supplier Payments
- VAT Collected / VAT Paid

### Optional (shown for all, used when provided):
- Labour / Staff Cost — separate from OPEX. Enables Prime Cost for restaurants, labour ratio for services.
- Previous month data (revenue, profit, cash, AR, inventory, AP) — enables trends + cash bridge
- YTD data (revenue, COGS, OPEX, profit, starting cash) — enables more accurate DSO/DIO/DPO

### NOT collecting (operational data we can't get from P&L):
- Covers, transactions, visits, sessions, subscribers
- Shrinkage, scrap rate, utilization hours
- CAC, churn count, conversion rate
- These are mentioned as "investigation points" in the report
