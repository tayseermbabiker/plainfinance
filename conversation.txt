## What the improved report should do
A strong report for non‑finance users should follow the same logic a CFO uses to understand a company:

### 1. Start with a mini P&L  
This gives the reader a stable anchor before diving into ratios or cycles.

Include only the essentials:
- Revenue  
- Gross profit  
- EBITDA  
- EBIT  
- Net income  

This answers the first question every non‑finance person has:  
**“Did we make money?”**

### 2. Then interpret those numbers in plain language  
Use the inputs they provided to explain:
- Why EBITDA is what it is  
- What drove EBIT up or down  
- Why net income differs from EBIT (interest + taxes)  
- Whether margins are improving or shrinking  

This is where your “coming up for air” metaphor shines:  
You’re translating the P&L into oxygen.

### 3. After that, show operational health (what you already do well)  
This is where your current report starts, but it should be the *third* step, not the first.

Include:
- Cash conversion cycle  
- Inventory days  
- Payables days  
- Receivables days  
- Operating cash movements  

This answers:  
**“Is the core engine running smoothly?”**

### 4. Then show whether assets are dragging the business (Free Cash Flow)  
This is the missing piece.

Free Cash Flow (FCF) tells the user:
- Whether the business generates real cash after maintaining its assets  
- Whether growth is consuming cash  
- Whether the company can self‑fund or needs external money  

For non‑finance users, phrase it like:
- “After paying for the equipment and assets your business needs, here’s how much cash is left over.”  
- “This is the money you can actually use.”

### 5. Finally, show whether debt is suffocating the business (FCFF / interest burden)  
This is the second missing piece.

FCFF (or a simplified version) tells the user:
- Whether the business can cover its interest payments  
- Whether debt is manageable or dangerous  
- Whether financing is helping or hurting  

Plain-language interpretation:
- “Your operations generate enough cash to comfortably pay interest.”  
- “Interest payments are eating X% of your operating cash — this is a warning sign.”  
- “Your debt is currently sustainable / risky.”
