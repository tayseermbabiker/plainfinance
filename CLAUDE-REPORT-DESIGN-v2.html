10 Things to Investigate Data we can't calculate — but you should track
  • 🍽️
    What is your table turn rate, and has it changed?
    Revenue looks healthy, but if covers per service have dropped and average spend is higher, your kitchen is working just as hard for the same top line. Table turns directly affect how efficiently you use food inventory.
  • 📦
    Are there signs of kitchen waste or theft affecting food cost?
    A 36% food cost — above benchmark even for a full-service restaurant — can be caused by over-portioning, waste, or theft as much as by supplier price increases. A one-week physical stock count will tell you which it is.
  • ⭐
    What is your online review trend over the last 90 days?
    Customer sentiment is a leading indicator of future revenue. If reviews have dipped while sales held steady, a revenue softening may be coming in the next 1–2 months before it shows in your financials.
  • 👥
    Is staff turnover or overtime hours increasing?
    Labour is within benchmark now at 26%, but hospitality turnover can push this up quickly. If you're covering shifts with overtime rather than through headcount, the hidden cost will appear in next month's numbers.
  • 🏢
    When does your lease renew, and do you have a rough sense of the rate?
    Rent is currently steady inside operating expenses, but a renewal negotiation in the next 6–12 months could materially shift your cost base. Knowing this now means you can plan for it — or use current profitability to negotiate from a position of strength.
